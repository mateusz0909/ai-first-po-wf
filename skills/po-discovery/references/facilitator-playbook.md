# Facilitator playbook — how to actually run a discovery workshop

For a PO who hasn't facilitated much. Read once end-to-end, then keep the
"in-the-room moves" section open during the session.

## Your one job
Own the **process**, not the content. You are neutral on the answer. Success =
everyone contributed, the real problem is framed, and the room agrees what to
test next. You talking least is a good sign.

## Before the workshop

**Goal (write it down, share it up front):** e.g. "leave with a shared problem
statement and the one assumption we'll test first." Not "solve it today."

**Who to invite (cap ~8 — bigger rooms go quiet):**
- **The decider** — one person who can commit (sponsor). Exactly one.
- **Domain experts** — 2-4 who live the problem.
- **An end-user voice** — someone who represents whoever feels the pain. If you
  can't get one, say so out loud and mark it a risk.
- Not: spectators. Every seat contributes.

**Logistics:** 90 min default. Remote → Miro/FigJam board pre-built with the
frames labelled; in-person → wall + stickies + Sharpies (thin markers force
brevity). Send a 1-paragraph pre-read: goal, agenda, "come with the last time
this problem bit you."

**Prep yourself:** draft a *provisional* problem hypothesis from research — to
CHALLENGE in the room, never to present as the answer.

## The core moves (use these all workshop)

- **Timebox out loud.** "5 minutes, silent." Visible timer. Short boxes beat long
  ones — people fill whatever you give.
- **Write-first, then share.** Everyone writes on their own stickies in silence
  BEFORE anyone speaks. This is the single most important move: it kills
  anchoring, stops the loudest/most-senior person from setting the frame, and
  gets the quiet experts' best material. Use it for every generative step.
- **Round-robin to share.** Go person by person; one sticky each, rotate. No
  interrupting.
- **Dot voting to converge.** Everyone gets N dots (≈ items/3), vote silently,
  cluster winners. Decider votes LAST or in private so they don't anchor.
- **Affinity clustering.** Group similar stickies, name the clusters together.
- **Parking lot.** A visible frame for off-topic-but-valuable points. "Great —
  parking it, back to X." Nothing gets lost, nothing derails.
- **Silence is a tool.** After a question, count to 7 before rescuing it. People
  fill silence with thinking.

## Reframing questions (your toolkit)

- **5 Whys** — ask "why is that a problem?" up to 5 times to get from symptom to
  root. Stop when you hit something you can act on.
- **Symptom vs problem vs solution** — for every stated item ask: "is this the
  thing that hurts, or a sign of it, or already a fix someone picked?"
- **Mom-Test style** (past, not hypothetical): "Tell me about the last time this
  happened" beats "would you want…". Facts about behaviour, not opinions.
- **Impact-map spine:** Why (goal/metric) → Who (actors) → How (impacts/behaviour
  changes) → What (deliverables). Deliverables are the LAST question, not first.
- **"How might we…"** to turn a framed problem into a solvable space without
  jumping to one solution.

## In-the-room problems → what to do

- **One person dominates / HiPPO anchors.** Switch to write-first + silent voting.
  "Let's capture everyone's independently first." Have the decider write privately
  and reveal last. Round-robin so airtime is even.
- **Someone brings a pre-baked solution.** Don't reject it — park it as a
  candidate, then ask "what problem does that solve?" and route it onto the
  impact map / assumption list. Solutions become hypotheses to test.
- **Rabbit hole / going long.** Timebox + parking lot. "Two more minutes, then we
  park the rest."
- **Dead silence.** Room too big, or no psychological safety. Break into pairs,
  write-first, or ask the most junior person first (seniors last).
- **Conflict / two camps.** Make the disagreement visible: "so we have two
  assumptions — A and B. We don't have to settle it now; which is riskier if
  wrong?" Convert the fight into an assumption to test. Data settles it later,
  not seniority.
- **Client insists their solution is the requirement.** Acknowledge, put it on
  the impact map as one branch, and show the map has other branches to the same
  goal. "Let's test the riskiest assumption behind it before we commit."

## Closing (last 10 min — never skip)
- Read back the **problem statement** — get nods.
- Show the **riskiest assumption** you'll test first.
- Name **owners + next step + date**.
- Thank them; say what happens next (prototype → they'll get to try it).

## After
Synthesize within 24h while memory is fresh → the three artifacts
(`artifact-templates.md`). Send the client a short recap
(`po-stakeholder-update`). Route the top assumption to `po-mvp`.

## Remote specifics
Pre-build the board. Assign a co-pilot to watch chat. Use the platform timer +
private voting. Cameras-on for the decider at least. Shorter boxes than
in-person — energy drops faster on video.
